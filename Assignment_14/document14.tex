	\section{Problem Statement}
	Let $T$ be the linear operator on $\vec{R}^2$ defined by 
	\begin{align}
		T(x_1, x_2) = (-x_2, x_1)
	\end{align}
	What is the matrix of $T$ in the ordered basis $\vec{B} = \cbrak{\alpha_1, \alpha_2}$, where $\alpha_1 = (1, 2)$ and $\alpha_2 = (1, -1)$ ?
	\section{Solution}
	Applying the transformations on $\alpha_1$ and $\alpha_2$ we get, 
	\begin{align}
		T(\alpha_1)  &= (-2, 1)\\
		T(\alpha_2) &= (1, 1)
	\end{align}
In order to write T($\alpha_1$) and T($\alpha_2$) in terms of $\alpha_1$ and $\alpha_2$, we row reduce the augmented matrix
\begin{align}
	\myvec{1 & 1 & -2 & 1\\2 & -1 & 1 & 1} 
	\xleftrightarrow[R_2 \leftarrow \frac{-R_2}{3}]{R_2 \leftarrow R_2 - 2R_1}
	\myvec{1 & 1 & -2 & 1\\0 & 1 & -\frac{5}{3} & \frac{1}{3}}\\
	\xleftrightarrow{R_1 \leftarrow R_1 - R_2}
	\myvec{1 & 0 & -\frac{1}{3} & \frac{2}{3} \\ 0 & 1 & -\frac{5}{3} & \frac{1}{3}}
\end{align}
Hence, the matrix $T$ in ordered basis $\vec{B}$ is
\begin{align}
	[T]_\vec{B} = \myvec{-\frac{1}{3} & \frac{2}{3} \\ -\frac{5}{3} & \frac{1}{3}}
\end{align}
